# Terror Turn — Preliminary Name and Trademark-Risk Screen

**Research date:** July 11, 2026  
**Status:** Preliminary business-risk screening only  
**Recommendation:** Keep with caution  
**Overall preliminary risk:** Moderate

> This document is not legal advice, a registrability opinion, or formal trademark clearance. Several official databases and storefronts use dynamic or regional interfaces that could not be conclusively interrogated in the research environment. “No conflict found” is not equivalent to legal availability.

## Executive conclusion

**Terror Turn may remain the preferred confidential working title.** The research found no obvious exact-name video game, board game, major entertainment property, stable numbered trademark record, or dominant common-law use for **Terror Turn** or its closest grammatical variants.

The title should **not** yet be treated as cleared for public announcement, storefront submission, domain purchase, merchandise, repository renaming, or expensive final branding.

### Main reasons

1. No indexed exact commercial title conflict was found for `Terror Turn`, `The Terror Turn`, `Terror’s Turn`, `Terrors Turn`, `Terror Turns`, `Turn of Terror`, `Turn for Terror`, or `Terror-Turn`.
2. No indexed exact storefront collision was found across the reviewed game and tabletop databases.
3. No stable numbered exact trademark application or registration was identified, but several authoritative search interfaces require a live professional rerun.
4. The strongest concern is distinctiveness: both words are common and closely describe the game’s horror and turn-based/board-game identity.
5. The strongest practical neighbor is **Terror Train**, primarily because of cadence, initials, and spoken-word confusion rather than identical meaning.

## Discoverability and branding observations

### Strengths

- Short, memorable, rhythmic, and alliterative.
- Clearly signals horror and board-game/turn structure.
- Flexible across rotating settings and anthology campaigns.
- Easy to type and reproduce without invented spelling.
- Supports the double meaning of a frightening player turn and “terror taking its turn.”

### Weaknesses

- Likely suggestive to descriptive-adjacent rather than highly distinctive.
- Spoken quickly, it may be misheard as **Terror Train**.
- Players may naturally reorder it as **Turn of Terror**.
- `Terror` creates broad horror and political/terrorism search noise.
- The wordplay may not translate equally well internationally.

### Presentation mitigation

- Use a consistent two-word lockup: **TERROR TURN**.
- Do not add apostrophes or hyphens.
- Avoid layouts that visually place `Turn` before `Terror`.
- Articulate both words clearly in voice-over and Underteller introductions.
- Pair the title with a more distinctive studio or franchise house mark over time.
