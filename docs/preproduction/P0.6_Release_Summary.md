## Rights and consent boundary

- A general recording release or absence of objection is not voice-cloning consent.
- Performer-linked voice replication or model training requires a separate explicit agreement or remains prohibited.
- Synthetic systems may render approved governed text only.
- Synthetic systems may not improvise rules, consequences, private state, targets, or new authoritative facts.
- Recognizable performer or protected-character imitation is prohibited.
- Confidential contracts, personal contact data, tax records, and restricted audition audio must not enter public GitHub.

## Accessibility and continuity boundary

- Critical speech is interruptible, replayable, captioned, text-persistent, and non-blocking.
- Every critical family includes a mechanically explicit plain-system equivalent.
- The game must remain understandable with Underteller voice and music disabled.
- Human departure, surrogate control, takeover, return, defeat, Tidebound transformation, and Restless continuation preserve the stable seat.
- Voice lines may not imply replacement, reset, respawn, automatic healing, inventory restoration, or player elimination.

## Boundaries

- No final Underteller name, script, performer, accent, model, provider, plan, or voice identity.
- No audition outreach, compensation, recording, cloning, model training, synthetic generation, final editing, processing, mix, or runtime integration.
- No private voice delivery over shared speakers.
- No localization casting or marketing/trailer use.
- Drowned Harbor remains design-only.
- Lantern House remains the sole production Tale.
- Codex/local Windows implementation and human listening/playtesting remain blocked until explicitly reopened by the project owner.
- Terror Turn and The Underteller remain provisional pending issue #7.

## Audition boundary

P0.6 intentionally stops before audition outreach, recording, or synthetic generation.

A later authorized audition package should:

- use the governed script packet;
- identify exact compensation and permitted use;
- use neutral candidate codes;
- preserve file identity and digests without publishing restricted raw audio;
- separate performance scoring from rights and continuity risk;
- compare character delivery to plain-system fallback;
- test against silence, ambience, SFX, and music;
- conduct human television-speaker, fatigue, caption, and gameplay-clarity review;
- avoid production, final-cast, or approval claims.
