## Accessibility boundary

- Critical speech is captioned, replayable, transcripted, persistent, text-scale safe, and non-blocking.
- Mechanically meaningful narrative cannot exist only in audio or a timed subtitle.
- Public transcripts and announcements contain public state only.
- Private companion, bargain, objective, role, inventory, route, or controlled-reveal content does not enter public history.
- Stable-seat continuity terminology prevents translation from becoming replacement, reset, respawn, or elimination language.
- Music-off, voice-off, and reduced-SFX play must retain the complete mechanical meaning through plain-system text and visual state.

## Pseudolocalization boundary

- `qps-ploc` is visibly test-only and is not a real language or supported player locale.
- Generated output is written to an explicitly chosen untracked path.
- Source manifests are not modified.
- Pseudolocalization tests expansion, accented glyphs, placeholders, wrapping pressure, and serialization only.
- It does not prove translation quality, typography, right-to-left support, accessibility, or in-context layout.

## Boundaries

- No final English copy.
- No translated locale.
- No localization, caption, accessibility, font, or machine-translation vendor.
- No final caption speed, segmentation, placement, background, transcript, screen-reader, focus, controller, right-to-left, or font implementation.
- No font files are selected or distributed.
- No accessibility-compliance claim.
- No production runtime integration.
- Drowned Harbor remains design-only.
- Lantern House remains the sole production Tale.
- Codex/local Windows implementation and human linguistic, readability, assistive-technology, and controller testing remain blocked until explicitly reopened by the project owner.
- Terror Turn and The Underteller remain provisional pending issue #7.

## Translation boundary

A later authorized translation package should:

- freeze a source version;
- export stable IDs, source text, placeholders, privacy, speaker, screenshots, and translator notes;
- preserve mechanical-equivalence keys and placeholder parity;
- use qualified review for mechanically authoritative and privacy-sensitive text;
- perform linguistic and in-context review;
- test caption segmentation, transcript, focus, text scale, overflow, fonts, and right-to-left behavior;
- record unresolved risks and bounded dispositions;
- avoid presenting machine-assisted drafts as approved translations.
