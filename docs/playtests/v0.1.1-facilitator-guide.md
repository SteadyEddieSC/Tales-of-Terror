# v0.1.1 Playtest Facilitator Guide

This checklist prepares a household or remote-observed session; it does not claim that one has occurred.

## Preflight

- Use the reviewed Godot 4.7.1 build at a 16:9 display setting.
- Confirm the title says `TERROR TURN` and X/H opens help.
- Connect intended controllers before play; keyboard is a development fallback.
- Decide whether phones are omitted or used only as optional companions.
- Tell players that shared-screen private reveal asks others to look away.
- Do not record names, contact information, roles, objectives, room codes, network details, or device IDs.

## Coverage matrix

| Group | Recommended focus | Input/phone variants |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 seat | cooperative fallback, self-guidance, reset | controller; keyboard fallback; no phone |
| 2 seats | cooperative fallback, handoff/privacy cues | two controllers; optional one phone |
| 3–4 seats | hidden-mode onboarding, prompt/vote progress, reconnect | controllers; no-phone and optional-phone runs |
| 5–8 seats | readability, eligible-seat progress, waiting, afterlife | controller mix; optional companion subset |

## Observe without coaching first

- Can players join, leave, and understand stable seats?
- Do mode/fallback, briefing, objective, stage, and expected input read clearly?
- Can players distinguish prompt/vote progress without seeing private choices?
- Are controller and keyboard equivalents discoverable?
- Does disconnect/reconnect preserve ownership without substitution?
- Do help, pause, and protected-reset consequences make sense?
- Can a defeated player identify continued afterlife participation?
- Are ending, report export, rematch, and return-to-title choices clear?
- Note pacing, confusion, privacy discomfort, and moments of delight without treating one session as balance certification.

## Report export

At the ending, press X/H, move to page 4, and press A/Enter. The game writes one JSON file and one Markdown summary under its local `playtest_exports` user-data folder. Confirm the success message. The report contains public/aggregate progress only; inspect before sharing. A write failure is visible and does not change the tale.

## Post-session questionnaire

1. From 1–5, how clear was joining and starting?
2. What did you think the public objective was?
3. When did you know whose input was expected? When was that unclear?
4. Were prompt/vote progress and private-reveal directions comfortable?
5. Could you find help, pause, reconnect, and reset guidance?
6. Did any text feel too small or dense from your actual seating distance?
7. Did defeated/afterlife participation remain meaningful?
8. Was the ending/rematch/report flow clear?
9. Where did the session feel slow, confusing, tense, or fun?
10. What one bounded presentation change would most improve the next session?

## Evidence classification

Automated tests prove deterministic contracts and privacy filters. Repository screenshots support remote visual review. Neither certifies physical controller compatibility, phone behavior, household Wi-Fi, TV-distance readability, native 4K, duration, balance, fun, accessibility with specific assistive technology, or long-session stability. Record those only after the corresponding real observation.
