# AI Media Production and Provenance Guide

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Preproduction workflow guidance  
**Reviewed:** July 23, 2026  
**Purpose:** Define a cost-conscious workflow for concept art, graphics, audio, music, video, and temporary production assets while preserving provenance and replacement options

## 1. Recommended stack

### Existing tools first

Use existing ChatGPT and Google AI access before purchasing another subscription.

Recommended division:

- **ChatGPT image generation:** original concept studies, iterative art direction, image editing, icon exploration, board mockups, character studies, and prompt refinement.
- **Gemini image tools:** alternate concept exploration, multi-reference image work, text-heavy mockups, and comparison passes.
- **Google Flow or Gemini video:** short mood clips, camera-motion tests, High Water animatics, lighthouse-beam studies, and storyboard visualization.
- **Google music generation:** temporary mood and motif experiments when included by the current Google AI plan.
- **Repository documentation:** scripts, prompts, negative constraints, shot lists, asset IDs, and provenance records.

Google AI plan names, storage amounts, credits, and limits change. Confirm the account's current benefits in Google One before planning a large generation batch.

### First optional paid service: ElevenLabs Starter

Recommended only when the project is ready to prototype Underteller delivery or generate a focused sound-effect set.

As reviewed July 23, 2026, ElevenLabs lists a Starter plan at $6 per month with a commercial license, 30,000 monthly credits, voice design, sound effects, music commercial use, and image/video access. Credits are shared across products.

Best uses:

- temporary Underteller voice auditions;
- line-delivery and pacing tests;
- bells, water, fog, doors, footsteps, lighthouse machinery, and Restless effects;
- short ambient loops;
- pronunciation and performance comparisons;
- non-final music sketches.

Do not clone a real person's voice without explicit permission. Do not imitate a recognizable actor or commercial horror host.

### Optional production-safety service: Adobe Firefly

Use when a visual asset is moving from exploratory concept toward production-intent graphics and the project benefits from Adobe's licensed/public-domain training posture and Content Credentials.

As reviewed July 23, 2026, Firefly Standard is listed at $9.99 per month with 2,000 credits, standard image tools, premium media access, and allocations for short videos and sound effects. Adobe states that outputs from Adobe Firefly models can be used commercially; partner-model outputs require separate judgment.

Best uses:

- vector-like icons and graphic elements;
- clean marketing-layout exploration;
- background extension and object cleanup;
- production-intent visual alternatives;
- Content Credentials and source transparency;
- storyboard and mood-board organization.

### Optional video specialist: Runway

Do not subscribe initially if Google Flow already meets the need.

As reviewed July 23, 2026, Runway offers a free one-time credit pool and a Standard tier listed at $15 monthly or $12 monthly when billed annually. Standard includes 625 monthly credits, multiple image/video models, upscaling, and no watermarks.

Use Runway only when a concrete limitation in the included Google workflow is identified, such as:

- character or environment reference consistency;
- a particular video-editing operation;
- controlled image-to-video motion;
- upscaling or finishing workflow;
- model comparison for one approved animatic.

Subscribe for one focused production month rather than maintaining it indefinitely.

## 2. Current cost recommendation

### No additional cost phase

Use:

- ChatGPT for design, prompt engineering, concept images, and image editing;
- current Gemini and Google AI benefits for additional images, video, music, and storyboards;
- free audio editing tools such as Audacity for trimming and cleanup;
- free image tools such as Krita, GIMP, or Inkscape for human-authored edits;
- repository storage for scripts, prompts, metadata, and approved compressed source assets.

### Low-cost audio month

When P0.4 begins, subscribe to ElevenLabs Starter for one month, generate a bounded Underteller audition and Drowned Harbor SFX pack, download source-quality files, document provenance, then decide whether continued access is justified.

### Production-art month

When selected graphics need cleaner commercial provenance or vector/layout work, use one month of Adobe Firefly Standard or an existing Adobe entitlement.

### Specialist-video month

Use Runway Standard for one month only if a defined shot list cannot be achieved with the current Google AI plan.

## 3. Google AI workflow

The project cannot invoke the owner's Gemini subscription directly from ChatGPT. The workflow is:

1. Define the asset brief and stable asset ID in this repository.
2. Create a carefully bounded prompt and negative constraints.
3. Paste the prompt into Gemini, Flow, or another selected Google tool.
4. Save the original output and generation metadata.
5. Upload the selected output for review or commit it through an approved asset workflow.
6. Record human edits and classification.
7. Keep the asset replaceable until production approval.

Google Flow may store prompts, uploads, generated outputs, and related metadata, and its privacy settings should be reviewed before uploading confidential or unreleased material. Do not upload secrets, credentials, private player data, or material that cannot be exposed to service review.

## 4. Asset classifications

Every asset receives one status:

- **Reference only:** inspiration or composition discussion; never shipped.
- **Concept:** generated exploration that may inform later work.
- **Prototype:** may appear in internal builds but is clearly temporary.
- **Production candidate:** selected for legal, artistic, and technical review.
- **Production approved:** reviewed, edited, sourced, licensed, and accepted for shipping.
- **Rejected:** retained only when needed for provenance or decision history.

AI output is never automatically Production Approved.

## 5. Required provenance record

Every generated or externally sourced asset should record:

- stable asset ID;
- filename and repository path;
- purpose;
- Tale, screen, character, item, or event association;
- classification;
- creation date;
- generator and service;
- model/version where shown;
- account or plan category, without storing credentials;
- complete prompt;
- negative constraints;
- reference images and rights status;
- generation seed or job ID where available;
- human edits and editing tools;
- source and export dimensions;
- source and export formats;
- licensing and commercial-use notes;
- attribution requirements;
- AI watermark or Content Credential status;
- reviewer and approval decision;
- replacement plan.

## 6. Visual-generation rules

- Request an original modern storybook/cel-shaded horror language using project-owned descriptive traits.
- Do not request direct imitation of a living artist, named commercial game, film, studio, or distinctive franchise style.
- Do not upload copyrighted reference art unless the project has the right to use it for that purpose.
- Keep the provisional wordmark as live text, not baked into art.
- Generate clean layers or isolated objects when practical.
- Preserve room for UI and television-safe framing.
- Create Spooky, Grim, and Gore & Dread variants only where presentation actually differs.
- Use human editing to establish protectable composition, shape language, color decisions, and final expression.

## 7. Voice and audio rules

- Use Voice Design or licensed stock voices rather than cloning a known performer.
- Record the selected voice name/model and settings.
- Mark all synthetic performances as temporary until casting is reviewed.
- Keep dry voice, processed voice, and project mix as separate files.
- Preserve text scripts and line IDs.
- Generate multiple delivery speeds for living-room readability tests later.
- Do not use synthetic delivery to claim final acting quality.
- Keep sound effects as separate one-shots, loops, and stems when possible.
- Do not rely on generated music or SFX without reviewing commercial terms for the exact service and subscription tier used.

## 8. Music guidance

For early preproduction:

- generate mood sketches rather than final soundtrack tracks;
- define tempo, instrumentation, dynamic arc, looping behavior, and interactive layers;
- avoid prompts naming active musicians or copyrighted songs;
- store prompts and exports;
- treat AI music as temporary until licensing and artistic direction are reviewed;
- prefer commissioned or clearly licensed final music if budget later permits.

## 9. Video and animatic guidance

Use generated video for:

- motion studies;
- camera tests;
- transitions;
- atmosphere reels;
- High Water board transformation concepts;
- internal pitch clips;
- trailer storyboards.

Do not treat generated video as final gameplay footage or misrepresent it as an implemented feature.

Every generated clip should be labeled internally as concept, prototype, or production candidate.

## 11. Tool-selection rule

Purchase or activate a new service only when:

1. a named preproduction deliverable cannot be completed well with existing tools;
2. a focused one-month generation plan exists;
3. expected outputs and quantity are defined;
4. licensing and privacy terms have been reviewed;
5. source files and provenance will be preserved;
6. the service can be cancelled after the batch.

## 12. Current recommendation

Use ChatGPT plus the owner's existing Google AI access for P0.2 and most of P0.3. Add one month of ElevenLabs Starter for P0.4. Consider Adobe Firefly Standard for production-intent graphic cleanup and Runway Standard only if Google Flow cannot satisfy a defined video shot list.

This keeps incremental media cost near zero until a concrete quality or licensing requirement justifies a focused subscription.
