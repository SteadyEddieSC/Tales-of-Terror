## 2. Official license rights found

Audio Hero's current end-user license describes its music and sound effects as royalty-free content licensed through a lifetime, non-transferable synchronization license.

The listed permitted productions include:

- computer games;
- software applications;
- film and video;
- websites and multimedia;
- broadcasts and presentations.

The license permits modification of length, pitch, and format for use in an authorized production.

The license does not transfer copyright ownership and does not permit raw-content resale or redistribution.

## 3. Ambiguity requiring written preservation

Audio Hero's general subscription FAQ says productions created while a subscription is active remain licensed after cancellation, while downloaded content is not licensed for new use with an inactive subscription.

Audio Hero has separately stated in public customer-support responses that:

- Humble Bundle access is recognized as a valid bundle rather than an ordinary expired subscription;
- downloaded Humble Bundle sounds are licensed perpetually;
- an ongoing paid subscription is not required for continued use of those bundle downloads.

Because the public EULA and general subscription FAQ can be read differently without the bundle-specific clarification, this project must preserve bundle-specific evidence before relying on the content.

## 4. Required confirmation before purchase or production use

Before purchase, save or obtain:

1. the Humble product page stating commercial or perpetual rights;
2. the Audio Hero EULA in effect on the purchase date;
3. the bundle-specific FAQ or written support clarification;
4. confirmation that downloaded Humble assets may be added to new game builds after bundle access or download credit expires;
5. confirmation that editing, looping, mixing, and format conversion are permitted;
6. confirmation of the redemption and download deadlines;
7. confirmation of whether the full catalog is accessed through credits, playlists, subscription time, or included archives;
8. confirmation that the final downloaded WAV or MP3 files may be stored locally for project use;
9. confirmation that raw audio files may not be placed in public GitHub or redistributed outside the compiled production.
