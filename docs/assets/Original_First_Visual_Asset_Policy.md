# Original-First Visual Asset Policy

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active preproduction policy
**Applies to:** Concept art, characters, environments, props, icons, UI, animation, textures, visual effects, trailers, store art, and promotional material

## 1. Core rule

Terror Turn should develop a recognizable visual identity rather than look assembled from common marketplace packs.

Original assets are preferred for:

- principal characters;
- the Underteller;
- Tale-defining locations;
- signature monsters and transformed forms;
- board spaces and major mechanisms;
- key props and quest objects;
- faction marks;
- UI frames, typography treatment, and icon language;
- promotional key art;
- title and storefront presentation.

Marketplace, bundle, stock, and generated assets may support the project, but they do not automatically become final production art.

## 2. Acceptable uses of purchased or bundled assets

Third-party assets may be used as:

- composition and lighting references;
- greybox or blockout geometry;
- temporary development placeholders;
- material, brush, sound, or texture sources where licensing permits;
- background dressing that does not define the project identity;
- animation or rigging foundations;
- components in a substantially transformed kitbash;
- technical examples for shaders, particles, or import workflows;
- internal mood-board material;
- training resources.

## 3. Transformation requirement

A purchased or generated visual asset should not become a production candidate merely because it is licensed.

Before approval, evaluate whether the asset has received enough project-specific authorship through changes such as:

- silhouette redesign;
- proportion and shape-language changes;
- original texture or material work;
- custom lighting and rendering treatment;
- topology, geometry, or mesh modification;
- original animation and posing;
- integration into an original composition;
- custom iconography or framing;
- hand paint-over;
- authored wear, damage, symbols, or environmental storytelling;
- color, value, and readability adaptation to the visual language guide.

Changing only color, scale, or file format is normally insufficient for a signature asset.

## 4. Asset originality tiers

### Tier A — Signature original

Expected for the Underteller, Tale key art, major transformations, central mechanisms, and storefront imagery.

Sources may include commissioned work, internally created art, substantially authored AI-assisted work, or deeply transformed licensed components.

### Tier B — Project-authored derivative

A licensed, scanned, stock, or generated source has been substantially modified and integrated into Terror Turn's visual language.

Appropriate for many props, secondary environments, effects, and supporting UI elements.

### Tier C — Licensed supporting asset

Used largely as purchased, but limited to background, generic, or low-identity roles.

Examples may include minor furniture, vegetation, debris, utility icons, or production tools.

Tier C assets should not dominate a screenshot or become visually synonymous with the game.

### Tier D — Placeholder only

Used for development, blocking, testing, reference, or concept comparison.

Tier D assets require a replacement plan before production approval.

## 5. AI-assisted visuals

AI output is treated as source material or a draft until reviewed.

Each candidate must record:

- generator and model;
- prompt and negative constraints;
- reference-image rights;
- generation date;
- source output;
- human edits;
- licensing and usage terms;
- watermark or Content Credentials status;
- originality tier;
- replacement plan when not final.

Do not request direct imitation of a living artist, protected character, commercial game, film, or recognizable brand style.

## 6. Bundle and marketplace evaluation

A visual bundle is attractive only when it fills an approved need.

Before purchase, identify:

- exact assets likely to be used;
- proposed originality tier for each;
- file formats and Godot or Blender compatibility;
- license and team-seat limits;
- whether modification is allowed;
- whether AI input, training, style transfer, or reference use is prohibited;
- whether raw source files may be stored privately;
- whether the purchase is still worthwhile if signature art must be replaced.

The bundle's advertised asset count and MSRP are not purchasing evidence.

## 7. Public repository boundary

Do not commit purchased raw assets, license keys, download links, receipts, or proprietary source packages to the public repository.

The repository may contain:

- asset identifiers;
- provenance records;
- hashes;
- license summaries;
- approved derivative outputs when distribution is permitted;
- replacement and attribution notes;
- source-location instructions that do not expose credentials or restricted downloads.

## 8. Review questions

Before approving a visual asset, ask:

1. Would players recognize this as Terror Turn rather than a common asset pack?
2. Is the silhouette or composition distinctive?
3. Does it support the storybook-horror direction?
4. Does it remain readable on a living-room display?
5. Is it consistent with the Tale and Underteller visual language?
6. Are the source rights and transformation history documented?
7. Could another game using the same bundle produce a nearly identical screenshot?
8. Does the asset require replacement before marketing or release?

## 9. Current project preference

The project owner prefers original visual assets and wants to avoid a cookie-cutter appearance.

Accordingly:

- signature visuals default to Tier A;
- purchased packs are primarily evaluated for Tier B, Tier C, or Tier D use;
- a bundle is not rejected merely because it is common, but its most recognizable assets should not define the final presentation;
- cost savings are valuable only when they preserve or strengthen the game's identity.

## 10. Release boundary

This policy does not approve any current visual asset, generator output, marketplace package, or Humble Bundle purchase.

P0.3 must apply this policy to every Drowned Harbor visual brief and candidate asset.
