# ADR-0011 — Retain Terror Turn after preliminary name screening

- **Status:** Accepted as a working-title decision; professional clearance still required
- **Date:** July 11, 2026

## Context

A broad preliminary screen reviewed exact and similar names across indexed game storefronts, tabletop databases, entertainment/media uses, trademark systems, domain/handle signals, and discoverability concerns.

No obvious exact commercial title conflict, exact storefront collision, stable numbered exact trademark record, or dominant common-law incumbent was identified for **Terror Turn** or its closest grammatical variants. The search environment could not conclusively interrogate every live authoritative database, regional catalog, registrar, or social platform.

The main risks are not a discovered blocking exact match. They are the title’s moderate distinctiveness, the crowded use of `Terror`, possible spoken confusion with **Terror Train**, natural reordering as **Turn of Terror**, and unresolved domain/handle availability.

The working title is already visible in this public repository, so it is not confidential. The remaining boundary is commercial commitment rather than secrecy.

## Decision

Retain **Terror Turn** as the preferred provisional working title with a **Moderate** overall preliminary risk assessment.

Continue using the temporary repository name `SteadyEddieSC/Tales-of-Terror`. Do not make a formal commercial title announcement, rename the repository, buy domains, claim handles, open storefront/crowdfunding pages, file a trademark application, or commission expensive final branding until a live attorney-led knockout and comprehensive search supports proceeding.

Separately clear **The Underteller** because it may function as a character name, secondary brand, or series mark.

## Alternatives considered

1. **Replace the title immediately.** Rejected because the preliminary screen found no meaningful exact conflict warranting a restart.
2. **Treat the title as cleared.** Rejected because official live databases, common-law use, regional catalogs, and recent/unindexed filings remain unresolved.
3. **Add a generic modifier.** Rejected because words such as `Game`, `Chronicles`, `Adventures`, or `The` would add little legal distance and could weaken branding.
4. **Use The Underteller’s Terror Turn.** Deferred pending separate clearance; it is more distinctive but longer and permanently ties the title to the host.

## Consequences

- Public repository development may continue under Terror Turn without restarting naming work.
- Repository, domain, social, and storefront identity remain unchanged.
- Placeholder branding must remain inexpensive and replaceable.
- Counsel should review Classes 9, 28, 41, 42, 16, and 25 plus any classes driven by actual merchandise plans.
- A fallback-name cycle begins only if legal review, platform review, domain/handle constraints, or user testing reveals a meaningful problem.
