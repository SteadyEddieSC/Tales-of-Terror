# Drowned Harbor Underteller Performance Addendum

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** P0.6 preproduction addendum
**Depends on:** `docs/voice/Underteller_Voice_Direction_v1.md`
**Tale status:** Design-only
**Production approval:** None

## 1. Tale-specific voice character

In Drowned Harbor, the Underteller sounds like a host who knows that the town has prepared a place for the players but does not yet know which version of the town will survive them.

The performance should carry:

- cold maritime patience;
- fascination with names, records, and remembered obligations;
- respect for the Bellhouse and lighthouse as mechanisms rather than scenery;
- restrained sympathy for residents whose memories have become infrastructure;
- precise delight when players alter an expected sequence;
- gravity when water changes the board;
- moral ambiguity around preservation, release, escape, erasure, and propagation.

Avoid:

- pirate or sea-captain delivery;
- nautical slang as a default;
- exaggerated coastal dialect;
- foghorn vocal effects;
- constant wet whispering;
- treating Tidebound or Restless characters as mindless monsters;
- treating the Harbor as simply evil.

## 3. Stage delivery

### 3.1 Low-Tide Arrival

Mode blend:

- Host;
- Witness;
- intensity 1–2.

Delivery:

- spacious;
- curious;
- lightly welcoming;
- the town appears prepared rather than randomly abandoned;
- allow visual geography and P0.4 ambience to breathe.

The Underteller should not announce maximum danger immediately.

### 3.2 Bellhouse Ledger

Mode blend:

- Host;
- Adjudicator;
- Revelator;
- intensity 2–4.

Delivery:

- procedural;
- exact;
- attentive to names and public marks;
- small pauses before counts, positions, and consequences;
- no secret Bellmarked foreshadowing.

The Bellhouse should feel like a lawful mechanism operating under impossible assumptions.

### 3.3 Lighthouse Council

Mode blend:

- Host;
- Tempter;
- Adjudicator;
- intensity 2–4.

Delivery:

- quieter;
- slower;
- several plausible futures held open;
- factual choice language receives the clearest stress;
- no vocal cue suggests the morally or mechanically correct direction.

Silence may be more effective than commentary during discussion.

### 3.4 High Water

Mode blend:

- Witness;
- Adjudicator;
- Revelator;
- intensity 3–5.

Delivery:

- active;
- reduced decorative language;
- public route and rescue consequences stated clearly;
- no shouting over the P0.4 Terror Turn cue;
- allow authored silence after the transformation before the first post-turn line.

The voice should distinguish danger, committed loss, and recoverable pressure.

### 3.5 Last Light

Mode blend:

- Adjudicator;
- Mourner;
- Revelator;
- intensity 3–5.

Delivery:

- sparse;
- grave;
- final choices stated without emotional coercion;
- seat and faction outcomes attributed separately;
- no universal victory or defeat framing when results are mixed.

## 4. Bellhouse language

Bellhouse lines should favor:

- count;
- index;
- mark;
- record;
- position;
- answer;
- witness;
- ring;
- name;
- silence.

Use these terms accurately.

Avoid overusing:

- toll;
- doom;
- death knell;
- curse;
- prophecy;
- traitor.

An ordinary strike is not automatically a death sentence. The extra ring represents an unresolved public position, not proof of one hidden player.

## 5. Lighthouse language

Lighthouse lines should favor:

- direction;
- lens;
- signal;
- turn;
- carry;
- inland;
- seaward;
- extinguish;
- receive;
- return.

The lighthouse is a memory-guidance machine.

Avoid:

- heroic beacon clichés;
- salvation as a guaranteed result;
- science-fiction transmission vocabulary;
- implying a destination is correct before commitment;
- describing the light as purely benevolent.

## 6. Harbor bargain delivery

Mode:

- Tempter;
- intensity 2–4.

The Harbor's bargain should sound:

- exact;
- patient;
- contractual;
- possible to refuse;
- materially grounded in water, glass, brass, names, routes, or records.

The Underteller may frame the public offer but may not expose private terms over shared speakers.

Acceptance:

- binding, not triumphant.

Refusal:

- neutral, not rewarded or punished through tone.

Debt collection:

- inevitable because of the accepted term, not because the Underteller is angry.

## 7. Bellmarked delivery

Before public reveal:

- no unique vocal warmth, suspicion, pronunciation, pause, or menace may identify a Bellmarked seat.

After public reveal:

- Revelator mode;
- morally ambiguous;
- preservation may be sincere, selfish, catastrophic, or mixed;
- do not use generic traitor or villain delivery;
- public facts only.

The line should reveal allegiance without assigning an unproven motive.

## 8. Tidebound delivery

Tidebound characters remain active, intelligent stable seats.

Before public transformation:

- no vocal foreshadowing from private eligibility.

After public transformation:

- Witness or Revelator mode;
- emphasize continuity and altered capability;
- avoid zombie, infection, infestation, or possession shorthand unless a public mechanic explicitly uses it;
- Gore & Dread may use more physical language but not dehumanizing delivery.

## 9. Restless continuation delivery

Bell-Witness, Drowned Guide, and Lighthouse Guardian are continuation forms, not player elimination screens.

The Underteller should communicate:

- the old action set changed;
- the stable seat remains active;
- new public agency exists;
- private objectives remain private unless revealed;
- grief and usefulness may coexist.

Avoid:

- farewell language;
- “you are out” framing;
- funeral performance;
- implying automatic sacrifice;
- treating the continuing seat as an NPC.

## 10. Seat-control delivery

Departure, surrogate activation, human takeover, and return are system continuity events.

Delivery:

- calm;
- brief;
- non-supernatural;
- nonpunitive;
- intensity 1.

The Underteller may acknowledge a returned player through an authored public recap, but must not reveal private inherited information over shared speakers.

## 11. Pressure commentary

Director commentary may react to public pressure bands:

### Quiet

- observational;
- curious;
- no false urgency.

### Watchful

- slightly tighter rhythm;
- note public instability without prediction.

### Strained

- less warmth;
- emphasize legal responses and visible consequences.

### Critical

- minimal ornament;
- exact choice or danger;
- no volume-only escalation;
- no private-target implication.

The Underteller should not comment on every pressure change.

## 12. Ending delivery

### Harbor Sealed

- containment and cost;
- no uncomplicated triumph.

### Drowned Released

- relief with remembered weight;
- no sentimental heaven shorthand.

### Last Lifeboat

- survival and absence attributed separately;
- no universal escape victory.

### Harbor Rises

- public authority and consequence;
- beautiful danger permitted;
- no villain monologue.

### Light Comes Home

- signal departure and public carrier attribution;
- no claim about future events.

### Names Erased

- subtraction and uncertain residue;
- no claim that restoration is impossible unless mechanics establish it.

### Mixed Outcome

- each public seat and faction result receives its own factual weight;
- no single emotional verdict.

## 13. Profile examples

All examples are style samples, not final script.

### Public route warning

Spooky:

> “The lower street has begun to reconsider its shape.”

Grim:

> “The lower street is giving way. Choose another route, or secure it now.”

Gore & Dread:

> “The lower street splits under the water. Secure it now, or cross what remains.”

The mechanical options must remain equivalent.

### Stable-seat return

All profiles:

> “Your seat remained. The Harbor did not leave it untouched.”

The private recap follows only on an authorized surface.
