# Drowned Harbor Audio Direction

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** P0.4 preproduction direction
**Tale status:** Design-only
**Production approval:** None
**Scope:** Ambience, sound effects, interaction cues, transition cues, and public system audio
**Deferred:** Final music direction, final Underteller casting, recorded dialogue, implementation, mix approval, and human listening tests

## 1. Sonic north star

Drowned Harbor should sound like **a town remembering itself through water, timber, bells, rope, glass, and damaged records**.

The audio world should feel:

- old but mechanically purposeful;
- maritime without becoming a generic pirate soundscape;
- supernatural without constant whisper clichés;
- dark and dreary without becoming a flat low-frequency drone;
- tactile enough to support the storybook-diorama visual language;
- readable enough that important actions remain understandable on ordinary television speakers;
- restrained enough that the Underteller and player decisions remain dominant.

The Harbor does not need to be loud to feel dangerous. Its most effective sounds should imply that familiar materials are behaving with incorrect memory.

## 2. Core audio principles

### 2.1 Mechanical truth first

Audio may emphasize a state change, but it may not contradict authoritative mechanics.

Examples:

- A bell-warning cue may indicate that a public mark exists, but may not imply automatic death.
- A route-closure cue may communicate danger, but may not sound like a route is permanently destroyed when recovery remains possible.
- A seat-departure cue may acknowledge control transfer, but must not imply the stable seat was removed.
- A Tidebound transformation cue may signal a public form change, but may not reveal a latent private condition before authorized revelation.

### 2.2 No sound-only required information

No required clue, warning, route state, ownership change, private reveal, or confirmation may exist only in audio.

Critical and mechanically meaningful sounds require:

- visible state change;
- caption or text equivalent where appropriate;
- icon, route, seat, or prompt reinforcement;
- mono-safe presentation;
- reduced-dynamics behavior.

### 2.3 Speech and decisions remain dominant

The Underteller, plain-system announcements, and decision prompts outrank ambience and most effects.

When voice or critical text presentation begins:

- ambience reduces in level and density;
- long tails shorten or duck;
- repetitive mechanisms yield;
- nonessential transient effects delay when possible;
- one-shot cues do not mask the first or final words of a spoken line.

### 2.4 Original-first sound identity

Signature sounds default to original design or substantially transformed original recordings.

Signature sounds include:

- the Bellhouse bell family;
- the unresolved extra ring;
- the lighthouse lens mechanism;
- the High Water Terror Turn;
- Harbor bargains and debt collection;
- Tidebound transformation;
- Bellmarked revelation;
- Restless continuation forms;
- the Drowned Archive;
- Last Light final-state punctuation.

Licensed libraries may provide raw water, wind, timber, rope, metal, cloth, glass, and impact sources when the license permits transformation and project use. An unmodified stock cue should not define a signature event.

## 3. Sonic material language

### 3.1 Bells

The Bellhouse family should combine:

- oxidized bronze or brass body;
- slightly unstable pitch center;
- long but controlled resonance;
- wood, rope, and tower vibration;
- subtle secondary tones that suggest indexed names;
- enough low-mid information for television speakers;
- no church-bell grandeur by default.

The unresolved extra ring should sound related to the main bell but mechanically wrong. It should not simply be louder, lower, or reversed.

Possible distinctions:

- a partial strike arriving before the main transient;
- a resonance that begins from silence without a visible impact;
- one missing harmonic replaced by wet glass or rope vibration;
- a delayed reflection that resolves from a different spatial direction;
- a double decay whose second tail does not match the room.

### 3.2 Water

Water should communicate state, route, depth, and supernatural pressure through several textures:

- shallow tide pools and thin reflective movement;
- mud suction and water entering cracks;
- current through broken structures;
- interior flooding and submerged resonance;
- heavy distant sea pressure;
- restrained impossible sounds within water.

Avoid constant crashing surf. The most important water in Drowned Harbor is often water where streets, rooms, and memories should be.

### 3.3 Timber and structures

Timber should sound swollen, salt-stiff, and under uneven load.

Use:

- fiber strain;
- deep joint movement;
- wet steps;
- hollow dock resonance;
- rope against wood;
- slate and fastener movement;
- selective collapse debris.

Avoid generic haunted-house door creaks for every interaction.

### 3.4 Rope

Rope is a major mechanical material.

It should communicate:

- tension;
- rescue load;
- bell indexing;
- lifeboat restraint;
- route stabilization;
- imminent failure.

Bell ropes, rescue ropes, and mooring lines should belong to one family but remain distinguishable by environment, load, and attached mechanism.

### 3.5 Glass and lens mechanisms

The lighthouse family should use:

- thick cloudy glass resonance;
- brass ring movement;
- indexed clicks;
- friction and slow bearing motion;
- directional beam sweeps represented without science-fiction energy sounds;
- crack pressure that feels structural and mnemonic.

### 3.6 Paper, ink, and records

Records should sound swollen, fibrous, and physically resistant.

Use:

- wet page separation;
- thick paper flex;
- feathered scraping;
- brass index tabs;
- water droplets in bindings;
- subtle repeated handling that suggests several histories occupying one page.

Avoid magical sparkle or generic spellbook sounds.

### 3.7 Human and post-human presence

Drowned residents, Tidebound forms, and Restless continuations should not depend on stock monster growls.

Prefer:

- layered breath without intelligible speech;
- clothing and water movement that arrives slightly out of sync;
- doubled footfalls;
- body-weight changes;
- memory fragments expressed as texture rather than hidden dialogue;
- spatial absence, interruption, or impossible continuation.

No generated or recorded intelligible private dialogue belongs in P0.4.

## 4. Stage ambience

### 4.1 Low-Tide Arrival

The town is exposed and listening.

Core layers:

- distant returning waterline;
- shallow pool movement;
- mud suction and crack release;
- isolated timber strain;
- weather-torn canvas;
- occasional far bell resonance without a full strike;
- wind moving around empty structures;
- minimal animal presence.

The ambience should leave enough space for curiosity and orientation.

### 4.2 Bellhouse Ledger

The town begins indexing the players.

Core layers:

- rope movement under no visible hand;
- tower wood and brass vibration;
- page fibers and index tabs;
- distant structures answering the bell;
- controlled low resonance after public name events;
- localized quiet before major ledger choices.

### 4.3 Lighthouse Council

The group approaches a machine that can guide memory.

Core layers:

- high exterior wind;
- slow lens bearings;
- cloudy glass pressure;
- brass indexing;
- directional beam sweep;
- tower structure movement;
- reduced town ambience below.

The Council prompt should create a deliberate listening space rather than add more layers.

### 4.4 High Water

The board changes from exposed town to active water network.

Core layers:

- advancing current through streets;
- debris and rope under load;
- interior water movement;
- lifeboat and dock strain;
- submerged impacts;
- distant bell resonance carried through water;
- Archive access or collapse layers when relevant.

High Water should feel more active but not become a constant wall of noise.

### 4.5 Last Light

The mix narrows to final mechanisms and consequences.

Core layers:

- reduced environmental breadth;
- lighthouse lens and bell relationship;
- controlled sea pressure;
- selected route or ending mechanism;
- very sparse structural movement;
- near-silence around irreversible confirmations.

Ordinary exploration ambience should recede.

## 5. Presentation profiles

Profiles change intensity and texture, not mechanics, timing windows, or required information.

### 5.1 Spooky

- clearer upper-mid detail;
- softer impacts;
- less explicit body texture;
- more storybook cloth, wood, paper, and wind;
- shorter distress tails;
- cleaner silence around prompts;
- danger implied rather than anatomically represented.

### 5.2 Grim

- default material weight;
- stronger low-mid water and structure pressure;
- clear injury and transformation texture;
- darker ambience without crushed detail;
- more pronounced storm and mechanical strain;
- restrained human breath and body-weight layers.

### 5.3 Gore & Dread

- localized wet anatomy, breath interruption, bone or tissue detail where justified;
- stronger physical transformation texture;
- no increase in gameplay volume solely for intensity;
- no constant gore bed;
- no sexualized audio;
- route, prompt, seat, and speech clarity remain unchanged.

## 6. Privacy and shared-screen rules

### 6.1 Public speaker output

Public speakers may present only:

- public board state;
- public hazards;
- public item and route actions;
- public faction revelations;
- authorized public transformations;
- public seat-control changes;
- public ending results.

### 6.2 Private information

Private roles, factions, objectives, bargains, latent transformations, and controlled handoff details must not be encoded into public audio.

Private-surface audio is deferred and requires a separate implementation and privacy review.

Default rule:

- private information uses visual controlled reveal;
- shared speakers remain neutral;
- no unique public cue identifies a hidden faction before reveal;
- no headset assumption is required for local play.

### 6.3 Seat continuity

Control changes should use calm, neutral cues.

A disconnect, surrogate activation, takeover, or return is not a defeat, punishment, or supernatural event unless the Tale separately authorizes one.

## 7. Mix hierarchy

Provisional bus order:

1. `Dialogue`
2. `Critical UI`
3. `Critical Tale SFX`
4. `Interaction SFX`
5. `Environment SFX`
6. `Ambience`
7. `Music` — reserved for P0.5

### 7.1 Dialogue ducking

During Underteller or plain-system speech:

- ambience and long environmental effects reduce first;
- noncritical one-shots yield;
- critical confirmation cues remain but avoid speech transients;
- the first and final words remain unmasked;
- no automated ducking behavior is approved until implementation and listening tests.

### 7.2 Dynamic range

P0.4 does not approve final loudness values.

It establishes these requirements:

- no required cue depends on a sudden extreme peak;
- presentation profiles do not increase raw loudness as their primary distinction;
- a reduced-dynamics or night-mode behavior must be definable;
- televisions and modest stereo speakers remain primary targets;
- headphones and surround systems may improve depth but cannot be required;
- mono compatibility is mandatory for critical cues.

### 7.3 Simultaneous events

When several events occur:

- one critical cue owns the foreground;
- repeated minor cues may group, delay, or suppress;
- ambience does not restart unnecessarily;
- identical transients receive controlled variation;
- no audio event may duplicate a state mutation.

## 8. Spatial behavior

Use four provisional spatial classes:

- `global_nonspatial` — UI, confirmations, system control changes;
- `board_localized` — landmarks, routes, hazards, public seat locations;
- `environment_bed` — broad ambience with authored stereo movement;
- `private_surface_deferred` — prohibited from shared speakers and not implemented in P0.4.

Board-localized audio should remain understandable when collapsed to stereo or mono.

Directional clues may support attention but cannot be the only route or objective indicator.

## 9. Loop design

Ambience loops should:

- avoid obvious repeated signature events;
- use layered stems or randomized sparse one-shots;
- preserve phase and mono compatibility;
- allow stage transitions without abrupt cuts;
- support reduced-density variants;
- exclude narrative events that should occur only once;
- leave space for dialogue and decisions.

A bell strike, named knock, transformation, bargain, or route failure should not be embedded permanently in a base loop.

## 10. Captions and visual redundancy

Captions should describe meaningful sounds without exposing hidden causes.

Examples:

- `[A bell rings once beyond the visible count.]`
- `[Water enters the lower street.]`
- `[The lifeboat mooring strains.]`
- `[The lighthouse lens begins to turn.]`
- `[A seat-control handoff completes.]`

Avoid captions that reveal:

- hidden faction identity;
- private objective;
- private bargain cost;
- latent transformation;
- unrevealed target selection;
- information not authorized for the public projection.

## 11. Master and derivative targets

Provisional source targets:

- 48 kHz master sample rate;
- 24-bit WAV source masters where available;
- lossless archive of approved source and edit sessions;
- engine-ready compressed derivatives generated later;
- stereo or mono according to governed brief;
- seamless-loop verification for looped assets;
- source, generator, prompt, license, edits, and disposition retained.

These are preparation targets, not a final engine import contract.

## 12. Generation and recording workflow

Potential source methods include:

- original field or Foley recording;
- original synthesis and sound design;
- AI-assisted generation with documented tool, model, prompt, and terms;
- licensed library sources transformed into project-specific composites;
- commissioned design with explicit project rights.

Before any paid service or bundle is used:

- the governed cue list identifies an actual need;
- commercial-game rights are verified;
- AI-input and redistribution restrictions are understood;
- raw-source public-repository rules are clear;
- the usable licensed subset justifies the cost;
- cancellation, download, or redemption deadlines are recorded.

## 13. First-wave signature families

P0.4 should govern briefs for:

- Lowest Tide ambience;
- Salt Market canvas and stall bed;
- Bellhouse ambience and bell family;
- lighthouse exterior and lantern-room mechanisms;
- Drowned Archive ambience and collapse;
- High Water street transformation;
- Last Light reduced ambience;
- ordinary bell strike and unresolved extra ring;
- ledger reveal and name indexing;
- rope rescue and mooring strain;
- lifeboat launch and break-free events;
- route warning and street collapse;
- current taking a public item;
- High Water Terror Turn signature;
- Harbor bargain offer, acceptance, refusal, and debt collection;
- Bellmarked public reveal;
- Tidebound public transformation;
- Bell-Witness, Drowned Guide, and Lighthouse Guardian activation;
- neutral seat departure, surrogate activation, takeover, and return cues;
- public ending punctuation without final score.

## 14. Human review questions deferred

When listening tests become available, evaluate:

- television-speaker clarity;
- speech masking;
- fatigue over a full Tale;
- loop repetition;
- frightening versus merely loud behavior;
- profile differentiation;
- caption usefulness;
- mono and reduced-dynamics behavior;
- whether the bell family remains recognizable without becoming irritating;
- whether water and mud layers become monotonous;
- whether the soundscape supports rather than overwhelms local conversation.

No P0.4 automation result substitutes for these tests.

## 15. Approval boundary

This document does not approve:

- final sound design;
- final mix or loudness;
- final bus implementation;
- final music;
- final Underteller voice;
- recorded dialogue;
- audio accessibility or television-speaker claims;
- marketplace or subscription purchases;
- AI-generated audio as production-ready;
- Drowned Harbor runtime integration;
- a second production Tale.
