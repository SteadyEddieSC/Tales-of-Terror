## Candidate F — ChatGPT `cgpt-v2.png`

### Strengths

- Best synthesis produced so far.
- Preserves the strong foreground causeway and traveler entry.
- Clearly distinguishes Bellhouse, Salt Market, Lifeboat Shed, and lighthouse.
- Lighthouse is dominant without eliminating the other landmarks.
- Geography is coherent enough to inform a future authored board.
- Dark maritime palette, restrained amber lights, and black tidal mud fit the primary mood.
- Storybook ink, watercolor texture, and controlled dimensional depth coexist successfully.
- Contains useful open sky for future provisional title-layout studies without embedding text.

### Weaknesses

- The lighthouse tower is slightly too clean and pale compared with the surrounding harbor.
- Bellhouse ropes read as numerous decorative strands rather than one understandable mechanism.
- The central market mass could use clearer stall organization and a more authored silhouette.
- The scene still needs one or two restrained supernatural impossibilities so it feels like a memory mechanism rather than only an abandoned tidal town.
- Some background houses remain same-family and could be simplified further.

### Disposition

Adopt as the **provisional Drowned Harbor visual north star** for P0.3. It is not final key art. Use it to guide subsequent lighthouse, Bellhouse, market, lifeboat, palette, and board-layout briefs. Limit future revisions to targeted corrections rather than further unguided whole-scene generation.

## Candidate G — Gemini `Gemini_2.png`

### Strengths

- Cleanest landmark and route readability.
- Strong hand-inked silhouette language.
- Salt Market, Bellhouse, Lifeboat Shed, travelers, and lighthouse are immediately identifiable.
- Excellent reference for the Spooky profile and simplified board-state presentation.
- Lower visual density could translate well to television-distance interaction layouts.

### Weaknesses

- Far too bright and low-contrast for primary key art.
- Material differentiation is weak.
- Bellhouse becomes almost as visually dominant as the lighthouse.
- The town feels melancholy and abandoned rather than supernatural and dangerous.
- The composition lacks tactile miniature depth and cinematic atmosphere.

### Disposition

Retain as the **Spooky-profile, silhouette, and readability reference**. Do not use it as the main key-art north star.

## Candidate H — Gemini `Gemini_3.png`

### Strengths

- Preserves Candidate G's clean geography and silhouettes.
- Adds stronger dread and nighttime atmosphere.
- Amber lighthouse beam reads clearly against the darker sky.
- The Salt Market receives useful visual identity.
- Could inform a high-contrast Grim board-state or storm-pressure presentation study.

### Weaknesses

- Heavy cyan/teal grading becomes more conventional fantasy-horror than the intended muted maritime palette.
- Red market fabric becomes a competing accent against the scarce-amber rule.
- Bellhouse remains too dominant relative to the lighthouse.
- Flat color grading does not provide the tactile depth or material specificity of Candidate F.
- The darker foreground begins to lose some route and traveler clarity.

### Disposition

Retain as a **Grim-profile and board-lighting reference**, not as the main key-art direction.

## Decision

P0.3 should stop broad key-art exploration and use Candidate F as the provisional visual north star.

Borrow only controlled qualities from the Gemini variants:

- Candidate G: clean contours, simplified silhouettes, and Spooky-profile value range.
- Candidate H: stronger storm pressure and readable amber-against-dark contrast.

Do not merge Candidate H's dominant cyan grade or red accent into the core palette.

## Targeted north-star corrections

Future Candidate F revisions should address only:

1. weather and salt-damage the lighthouse so it belongs to the same material world;
2. simplify Bellhouse ropes into a readable authored mechanism;
3. organize the Salt Market into a clearer central landmark;
4. reduce repetitive background housing;
5. add one or two subtle supernatural memory cues without adding clutter;
6. preserve the causeway, travelers, landmark geography, composition, and restrained amber lighting.

This decision establishes a provisional preproduction reference only. It does not approve final key art, final art direction, production assets, title treatment, storefront imagery, board readability, accessibility, implementation, or a Drowned Harbor production Tale.
