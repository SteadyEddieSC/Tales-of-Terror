# The Underteller Voice Direction

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** P0.6 preproduction direction
**Name status:** The Underteller remains provisional pending issue #7
**Production approval:** None
**Scope:** Performance identity, casting, delivery, privacy, accessibility, recording preparation, and review criteria
**Deferred:** Final casting, voice model selection, recording, processing, implementation, localization casting, and human listening tests

## 1. Voice identity

The Underteller is an **elegant, mechanically truthful master of ceremonies who happens to be dead**.

The voice should feel:

- intelligent without sounding academic;
- theatrical without becoming a stage caricature;
- amused without becoming flippant;
- threatening without constant anger;
- old without requiring a specific historical accent;
- supernatural without relying on distortion;
- capable of warmth that may be sincere, manipulative, or both;
- precise enough to explain rules and consequences clearly;
- restrained enough that silence remains meaningful.

The Underteller is not a generic horror narrator, pirate captain, carnival barker, villain monologuer, demon, or sarcastic streamer.

## 2. Core performance rule

**The Underteller may be dramatic about facts, but may not invent facts.**

Delivery may intensify:

- danger;
- consequence;
- uncertainty;
- public pressure;
- visible transformations;
- public bargains;
- final choices.

Delivery may not:

- imply an action has committed before authority confirms it;
- imply certain death when recovery remains possible;
- reveal hidden roles, factions, objectives, targets, inventory, bargains, or transformations;
- suggest one choice is mechanically correct when the game does not establish that;
- shame a player for requesting clarification, accessibility support, reconnecting, leaving, returning, or using game control;
- use emotional performance to override plain-system truth.

## 3. Vocal center

### 3.1 Register

Preferred center:

- adult low-middle speaking register;
- audible resonance without forced bass;
- flexible upper color for dry amusement and false reassurance;
- enough low support for authority on ordinary television speakers.

Avoid:

- artificial movie-trailer depth;
- constant whispering;
- gravel that obscures consonants;
- exaggerated elderly weakness;
- cartoon villain pitch;
- breathiness that creates listening fatigue.

### 3.2 Texture

Preferred texture:

- dry, articulate, lightly weathered;
- controlled breath;
- occasional soft smile in the voice;
- restrained roughness at moments of pressure;
- supernatural quality created mainly through timing, wording, and impossible knowledge of public events.

Signal processing should not be required to establish identity.

### 3.3 Accent

No final accent is approved.

Preferred casting behavior:

- broadly understandable international English;
- clear consonants and stable vowels;
- no accent selected solely as shorthand for evil, age, class, ethnicity, or foreignness;
- no pirate, exaggerated transatlantic, aristocratic parody, or imitation of a recognizable performer.

A natural regional accent may be acceptable when intelligibility and character fit remain strong.

## 4. Performance pillars

### 4.1 Polite menace

The Underteller often speaks as though hospitality and danger are part of the same social obligation.

Performance:

- calm invitation;
- precise courtesy;
- small pauses around consequences;
- no need to shout;
- threat arrives from certainty and timing.

### 4.2 Dry amusement

Humor should come from observation, not humiliation.

The Underteller may be amused by:

- improbable survival;
- elegant mistakes;
- rules behaving exactly as warned;
- a supernatural mechanism becoming inconvenient;
- players surprising the Director.

The Underteller may not mock:

- disability or accessibility needs;
- reading speed;
- language fluency;
- controller difficulty;
- age;
- a player leaving or reconnecting;
- personal identity;
- genuine confusion caused by unclear design.

### 4.3 Mechanical truth

Rules and state must remain understandable on the first listen.

Performance:

- emphasize the subject, action, and consequence;
- avoid burying the legal choice inside ornament;
- separate flavor from instruction through pause and cadence;
- pronounce identifiers consistently;
- reduce theatrical texture when the line carries critical gameplay information.

### 4.4 Selective intimacy

The Underteller may address a public seat or the group directly.

The voice should feel nearer during:

- invitations;
- bargains;
- acknowledgments;
- public marks;
- returned-player recaps;
- final questions.

Closeness must not reveal private state over shared speakers.

### 4.5 Earned intensity

Intensity should have room to grow.

The Underteller does not begin at maximum menace.

Provisional progression:

- invitation;
- observation;
- procedural unease;
- delighted pressure;
- grave consequence;
- final authority;
- quiet attribution.

## 5. Sentence behavior

Preferred lines:

- short to medium length;
- one central image;
- one mechanically relevant idea;
- controlled punctuation;
- active verbs;
- occasional parallel structure;
- rare repetition used intentionally.

Avoid:

- long lore paragraphs during active play;
- adjective stacks;
- constant rhetorical questions;
- archaic language for its own sake;
- fake quotations from unnamed ancient texts;
- repeated catchphrases;
- excessive ellipses;
- every line ending in a threat.

## 6. Delivery modes

### 6.1 Host

Used for:

- welcomes;
- setup;
- stage introductions;
- transitions;
- public recaps.

Delivery:

- clear;
- composed;
- lightly amused;
- forward-moving.

### 6.2 Witness

Used for:

- observing public choices;
- acknowledging transformations;
- noting survival or failure;
- explaining visible consequences.

Delivery:

- attentive;
- specific;
- less performative;
- emotionally responsive without judgment beyond public facts.

### 6.3 Tempter

Used for:

- public bargain framing;
- dangerous optional routes;
- uncertain opportunities.

Delivery:

- intimate;
- exact;
- patient;
- never misstates cost or certainty;
- acceptance and refusal remain equally valid responses.

### 6.4 Adjudicator

Used for:

- rule resolution;
- invalid action explanation;
- turn ownership;
- critical prompts;
- confirmation.

Delivery:

- plainest Underteller mode;
- firm but not scolding;
- minimal metaphor;
- legal options emphasized.

### 6.5 Mourner

Used sparingly for:

- public defeat;
- loss of residents;
- irreversible destruction;
- ending attribution.

Delivery:

- quiet;
- unsentimental;
- never treats stable-seat continuation as elimination;
- does not decide how the player should feel.

### 6.6 Revelator

Used for:

- public faction reveal;
- public form transformation;
- major truth completion;
- ending family commitment.

Delivery:

- controlled authority;
- clean reveal timing;
- no pre-reveal foreshadowing derived from private state;
- leaves space after the key fact.

## 7. Intensity scale

Each recorded line declares an intensity from 1 to 5.

### 1 — Conversational ritual

- relaxed breath;
- mild amusement;
- minimal supernatural weight.

### 2 — Watchful

- slightly slower pace;
- clearer consequence emphasis;
- restrained lower resonance.

### 3 — Pressured

- reduced warmth;
- tighter rhythm;
- stronger active verbs;
- no shouting.

### 4 — Grave

- slower, heavier certainty;
- fewer decorative choices;
- space before irreversible consequence.

### 5 — Final authority

- rare;
- controlled rather than loud;
- exact words;
- strong silence before or after;
- reserved for committed Terror Turns, final choices, and ending attribution.

Intensity may not reveal private state or substitute for visible pressure information.

## 8. Presentation profiles

Profiles change explicitness and texture, not mechanics or factual meaning.

### 8.1 Spooky

- clearer warmth;
- lighter threat texture;
- less bodily specificity;
- playful unease permitted;
- shorter distress emphasis;
- no childish delivery.

### 8.2 Grim

- default performance;
- cooler warmth;
- stronger consequence weight;
- restrained roughness;
- more silence around danger;
- no constant whisper or growl.

### 8.3 Gore & Dread

- retains Grim structure and timing;
- permits more explicit physical language where script policy allows;
- may add localized breath disruption after public body-horror events;
- does not become louder as its primary difference;
- no sexualized suffering;
- no prolonged agony performance unless separately reviewed and necessary.

## 9. Public and private information boundary

Shared-speaker Underteller lines may reference only authorized public state.

Publicly speakable examples:

- current stage;
- visible hazards;
- public marks;
- public transformations;
- public faction reveals;
- public seat-control changes;
- public objectives and ending results;
- coarse public pressure and truth state.

Not publicly speakable before authorized reveal:

- hidden role or faction;
- private objective;
- private bargain terms;
- private item or route knowledge;
- latent transformation;
- hidden target selection;
- private vote;
- controlled companion content;
- unrevealed ending carrier.

Private information defaults to controlled visual reveal. Private voice delivery is deferred and requires a separate privacy and device review.

## 10. Seat continuity language

Stable seats persist through human departure, surrogate control, takeover, return, defeat, transformation, and afterlife continuation.

Underteller lines must not say or imply:

- the character left when only the human controller left;
- a bot replaced the character;
- the seat reset;
- the returning player receives restored health or inventory;
- defeat removed the player from the Tale;
- a new human takeover created a new character.

Preferred framing:

- control changed;
- the seat remained;
- the Harbor continued to act upon it;
- the returning human inherits the seat exactly as it evolved.

Control-change lines should be calm and nonpunitive.

## 11. Plain-system fallback

Every critical Underteller line must have a plain-system text equivalent and, where needed, a plain spoken equivalent.

Plain-system delivery should be:

- neutral;
- concise;
- mechanically explicit;
- free of character performance that could obscure meaning;
- available when the Underteller voice is disabled or unavailable.

The game must remain playable with:

- Underteller voice off;
- music off;
- reduced SFX;
- captions on;
- plain-system text only.

## 12. Accessibility and intelligibility

Requirements:

- captions or text for all mechanically meaningful speech;
- consistent pronunciation of proper nouns and identifiers;
- no required distinction based only on whisper, accent, pitch, or emotional tone;
- no binaural or headset-only private instruction;
- no distortion required for comprehension;
- independent dialogue volume;
- dialogue ducking authority over music and ambience;
- alternate replay or transcript access for critical lines;
- interruption-safe prompts;
- no penalty for replaying or rereading a line.

Human listening tests are required before claiming television, hearing, fatigue, or accessibility performance.

## 13. Casting target

The ideal performer can:

- sustain authority at low volume;
- shift between warmth, amusement, pressure, grief, and finality without changing character;
- articulate invented names and mechanical instructions consistently;
- perform short variant lines without sounding disconnected;
- maintain identical factual meaning across presentation profiles;
- deliver multiple takes with controlled timing;
- avoid recognizable imitation;
- accept direction that prioritizes gameplay clarity over dramatic flourish.

No demographic identity is required for the role.

Casting should consider:

- vocal health;
- session stamina;
- availability for pickups;
- rights and compensation;
- consent for any voice processing or model training;
- localization and replacement strategy;
- whether the identity remains viable while the provisional name is unresolved.

## 14. Human performer rights

Before recording or model use, contracts must define:

- permitted game and marketing uses;
- DLC, updates, ports, trailers, and archival rights;
- compensation and pickup terms;
- credit preference;
- term and territory;
- editing and processing permissions;
- whether synthetic replication or voice-model training is prohibited, allowed, or separately licensed;
- approval requirements for new synthetic lines;
- revocation and breach handling;
- source-session storage and access;
- use after project, studio, or product renaming.

Silence or a general recording release does not constitute consent to voice cloning.

## 15. Synthetic or AI-assisted voice boundary

No tool or plan is approved in P0.6.

Before using a synthetic voice:

- commercial game rights must be verified for the current plan;
- the voice must not imitate a recognizable real performer without authorization;
- source-voice consent must be documented where applicable;
- model-training and data-retention terms must be understood;
- generated-line provenance must be recorded;
- the project must retain a replacement path;
- critical lines require human review;
- synthetic delivery may not infer private data or generate unscripted gameplay facts;
- the runtime may not improvise authoritative rules or consequences.

A synthetic system may render approved text. It may not author new authoritative state claims.

## 16. Recording target

Provisional source target:

- mono;
- 48 kHz;
- 24-bit WAV;
- dry recording without permanent reverb, pitch, radio, room, or supernatural effects;
- stable microphone position and gain;
- low room noise;
- several seconds of room tone per session;
- no clipping;
- consistent naming and slate policy;
- separate raw, selected, edited, and processed files;
- nondestructive edit history.

Final technical specifications require engineer review and test recording.

## 17. Take structure

For each line family, request:

- `A` — clear canonical take;
- `B` — slightly more intimate or amused;
- `C` — slightly more severe or restrained;
- pickups for pronunciation, timing, or factual emphasis;
- profile variant only when the script actually differs in explicitness.

Do not request random emotional variation that changes perceived rules.

## 18. File and line identity

Every recorded line should have:

- stable line ID;
- script version;
- Tale or global scope;
- trigger class;
- public/private class;
- delivery mode;
- intensity;
- presentation profile;
- pronunciation notes;
- expected duration range;
- take identifier;
- performer or model provenance;
- review status;
- approval boundary.

A filename should not depend on provisional marketing title alone.

## 19. Review criteria

Review each candidate for:

- factual accuracy;
- privacy safety;
- intelligibility;
- character consistency;
- timing;
- sentence stress;
- emotional fit;
- television-speaker resilience;
- caption match;
- breath and mouth-noise burden;
- fatigue over repeated variants;
- transition compatibility;
- absence of recognizable imitation;
- performer or model rights;
- ability to replace or localize later.

## 20. Prohibited performance patterns

Reject:

- constant evil whisper;
- constant shouting;
- trailer-announcer delivery;
- pirate captain delivery;
- carnival barker delivery;
- sing-song nursery horror;
- recognizable celebrity or character imitation;
- mocking player mistakes;
- sexualized menace;
- contempt for accessibility needs;
- vague mumbling used as atmosphere;
- improv that changes mechanics;
- hidden-state foreshadowing derived from private data;
- dramatic timing that obscures a legal choice.

## 21. Human review questions deferred

When listening becomes available, evaluate:

- whether the voice is understandable on ordinary television speakers;
- whether the character remains compelling at low volume;
- whether humor feels observant rather than cruel;
- whether stage intensity grows instead of beginning at maximum;
- whether repeated prompts become irritating;
- whether rule lines remain clear on the first listen;
- whether voice and music compete;
- whether profiles preserve the same factual meaning;
- whether long sessions cause fatigue;
- whether plain-system fallback feels complete;
- whether the voice remains original and not recognizably imitative.

## 22. Approval boundary

This document does not approve:

- final casting;
- final accent or demographic identity;
- a specific performer, model, vendor, plan, or tool;
- voice cloning or model training;
- final scripts;
- production recording;
- final processing, mix, loudness, or runtime integration;
- marketing or trailer voice use;
- localization casting;
- television, fatigue, or accessibility claims;
- The Underteller as a legally cleared final name.
