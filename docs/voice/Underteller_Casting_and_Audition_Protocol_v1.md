# Underteller Casting and Audition Protocol

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** P0.6 preproduction protocol
**Name status:** The Underteller remains provisional pending issue #7
**Production approval:** None

## 1. Purpose

This protocol defines a fair, rights-aware process for comparing human and separately governed synthetic voice candidates without treating an audition as production approval.

## 2. Audition tracks

Keep candidate tracks separate during review.

### Human performer track

- performer records the governed audition script;
- compensation and use terms are disclosed before recording;
- no synthetic replication or model training is permitted unless separately and explicitly licensed;
- audition audio is used only for evaluation unless a later agreement authorizes more.

### Synthetic or AI-assisted track

- candidate is generated only from approved text;
- model, service, plan, date, and settings are recorded;
- source-voice consent and commercial rights are documented where applicable;
- no recognizable performer imitation;
- no runtime improvisation or unscripted authoritative statements;
- generated files remain audition candidates only.

Human and synthetic candidates may be compared on performance criteria, but their rights, replacement risk, and operating constraints must be evaluated separately.

## 3. Audition packet

Each candidate receives the same packet:

- project and role summary;
- provisional-name notice;
- character direction;
- intended audience and shared-screen context;
- pronunciation guide;
- twelve audition lines;
- requested take structure;
- recording target;
- permitted and prohibited uses;
- submission deadline and contact method;
- compensation and pickup expectations for human performers;
- privacy and data-retention notice;
- statement that selection is not guaranteed.

Do not provide one candidate with private evaluation notes about another.

## 4. Audition line set

The line set should test distinct capabilities without requiring a full session.

Recommended twelve-line categories:

1. calm welcome;
2. concise rule explanation;
3. public hazard warning;
4. dry amusement;
5. Harbor bargain offer;
6. bargain refusal acknowledgment;
7. public faction reveal;
8. public transformation acknowledgment;
9. stable-seat departure or surrogate-control line;
10. returned-player public recap;
11. Last Light choice framing;
12. mixed ending attribution.

The audition script must not contain confidential plot content beyond what is needed to evaluate the role.

## 5. Requested takes

For each line:

- Take A — canonical clarity;
- Take B — slightly more intimate or amused;
- Take C — slightly more severe or restrained.

Candidates should not create new words or facts.

Minor natural contractions may be evaluated but are not automatically accepted as script changes.

## 6. Human recording submission target

Preferred audition submission:

- mono WAV;
- 48 kHz;
- 24-bit when available;
- dry and unprocessed;
- one file per line or one clearly marked continuous file;
- no music or sound effects;
- stable microphone position;
- no permanent noise reduction, pitch shifting, reverb, radio effect, or character processing;
- short room-tone sample;
- filename using candidate code rather than unnecessary personal information.

A lower-spec audition may still be reviewed when access or equipment would otherwise create an unfair barrier. Performance and intelligibility are evaluated separately from studio quality.

## 7. Candidate coding and review privacy

Assign each candidate a neutral review code.

Where practical, first-round reviewers should receive:

- candidate code;
- normalized line order;
- consistent playback level;
- rights-risk summary separated from performance scoring;
- no photograph or unnecessary demographic information.

Blind review cannot remove every bias, especially when a voice reveals natural characteristics, but it can reduce unrelated influence.

## 8. Evaluation rubric

Score each category from 1 to 5.

### 8.1 Mechanical clarity — 25%

- subject, action, and consequence are understandable;
- legal choices are not obscured;
- critical terms receive correct stress;
- performance does not imply false certainty.

### 8.2 Character fit — 20%

- elegant dead host;
- polite menace;
- dry amusement;
- selective warmth;
- not a generic horror narrator.

### 8.3 Range without character loss — 15%

- Host, Witness, Tempter, Adjudicator, Mourner, and Revelator modes remain one character;
- intensity grows without shouting;
- quiet lines retain authority.

### 8.4 Intelligibility and fatigue risk — 15%

- clear consonants;
- sustainable texture;
- no constant whisper or forced gravel;
- likely to remain listenable across repeated prompts.

### 8.5 Timing and editability — 10%

- clean phrase starts and endings;
- useful pauses;
- ability to meet duration direction;
- variants remain factually equivalent.

### 8.6 Originality — 5%

- no recognizable celebrity, character, game, film, or trailer-narrator imitation;
- role feels specific to the project.

### 8.7 Rights and continuity readiness — 10%

Evaluated outside blind performance scoring:

- clear rights;
- pickup availability;
- compensation feasibility;
- replacement and localization path;
- synthetic-model consent and data terms where applicable;
- acceptable Content ID, platform, and marketing-use risk.

A high performance score cannot override unacceptable rights or consent risk.

## 9. Automatic disqualifiers

Reject or pause a candidate when:

- the audition knowingly imitates a recognizable performer or protected character;
- a human audition was generated or cloned without disclosure;
- rights or source consent cannot be established;
- the candidate adds unscripted authoritative rules or private-state claims;
- the candidate uses hate speech, sexualized menace, or player-directed harassment;
- the service terms do not permit the intended commercial evaluation or game use;
- the candidate requires processing so heavy that intelligibility or replacement becomes impractical;
- the performer or provider does not consent to the actual requested use.

## 10. Review panel

Preferred review perspectives:

- narrative or creative direction;
- game design or rules clarity;
- audio direction or engineering;
- accessibility or usability review;
- project owner;
- later human playtest feedback when available.

One person may cover several roles in a small project, but score categories should remain distinct.

## 11. Review sequence

1. Verify submission completeness and rights disclosure.
2. Normalize playback for review without destructive processing.
3. Conduct first-round performance scoring.
4. Review rights and continuity risk separately.
5. Shortlist no more candidates than the project can meaningfully test.
6. Request targeted pickups using identical direction where possible.
7. Test shortlisted candidates against music-off, ambience, SFX, and music contexts.
8. Test plain-system fallback beside the character voice.
9. Conduct human listening and playtest review when available.
10. Record disposition and remaining risks.

## 12. Pickup protocol

A pickup request should identify:

- stable line ID;
- exact approved text;
- factual stress target;
- delivery mode;
- intensity;
- desired duration;
- pronunciation correction;
- what must remain unchanged;
- whether all takes or one take require replacement.

Avoid vague direction such as “make it scarier” when the actual need is timing, clarity, warmth, or consequence emphasis.

## 13. Synthetic candidate governance

Each synthetic audition record should include:

- provider and product;
- model or voice ID;
- plan or license class;
- generation date;
- full input text;
- style or delivery instructions;
- seed or reproducibility data when available;
- source-voice consent basis when applicable;
- commercial and marketing rights summary;
- model-training and retention terms summary;
- human edits and processing;
- replacement strategy;
- review disposition.

Synthetic candidates must be rendered from governed lines. They may not autonomously rewrite, improvise, or react to raw game state.

## 14. Human performer governance

Each human audition record should include:

- candidate code;
- performer contact record stored outside public GitHub;
- audition-use agreement;
- compensation status;
- recording date;
- equipment and environment notes where voluntarily provided;
- takes received;
- pickup availability;
- model-training consent status, defaulting to prohibited when absent;
- credit preference when relevant;
- review disposition.

Public repositories should not contain private contact information or contracts.

## 15. Comparison against plain-system fallback

Every shortlisted candidate must be compared with the neutral fallback for:

- clarity;
- timing;
- repeated prompt fatigue;
- rule comprehension;
- interruption behavior;
- caption match;
- dialogue ducking needs.

The character voice must add atmosphere without making the game less understandable than the fallback.

## 16. Decision outcomes

Allowed audition dispositions:

- `needs_revision`;
- `reference_only`;
- `rejected`;
- `preproduction_shortlist`;
- `deferred`.

P0.6 may not assign:

- `production_candidate`;
- `approved`;
- `final_cast`.

## 17. Records retained

Retain:

- audition packet version;
- script version;
- candidate code;
- file identity and digest;
- scoring sheets;
- rights summary;
- requested pickups;
- disposition;
- deletion or retention obligations.

Do not place confidential contracts, personal contact information, or restricted audition audio in public GitHub.

## 18. Approval boundary

This protocol does not authorize audition outreach, compensation, recording, synthetic generation, performer selection, voice cloning, production casting, or public use. Each requires a later explicit action and applicable agreement.
